Fit2Ear - Personalized Otoplastic from Smartphone Depth Camera

Tiny demostration see bottom.
With the integration of headphones into human life, numerous researchers have augmented ear-worn devices with state-of-the-art sensing technology for unobtrusive and ubiquitous health monitoring or activity recognition. To achieve these functions, advanced algorithms are often based on data-driven optimization algorithms, such as artificial neural networks. Therefore, the validity of the algorithms is greatly determined by the qualities of the datasets. Nevertheless, the research for earables is mainly based on universal earplugs, which could potentially generate undesirable noise during data collection, such as vibrations or even falling off. However, customizing earplugs for each subject is extremely time-consuming and expensive. Therefore, we build a smartphone-based hardware system for data collection in human ear modelling. Based on the pose graph optimization, a tightly coupled RGBD-Control information system for high precision modelling is proposed and evaluated. This dataset can also support other researchers in high precision human ear modelling.

Hardware infrastructure

To provide RGBD information, we employ an iPhone X smart phone (figure 2). It also equipped with a 9-axis IMU that provides acceleration, gyroscope and magnetometer information. Similar to Monocular SLAM, using these sensors, it is possible to estimate the trajectory of the phone and to register the point cloud obtained during the movement based on the trajectory. However, due to the low accuracy of RGBD point clouds and IMUs on the smart phone, and the fine structure of the human ear, it is difficult to precisely model the ear. In addition, since there is no global correction, errors in monocular systems accumulate as time progresses, becasue the estimation made in this system is always an iterative process based on previous estimation.
To this end, we use a Niryo robotic arm to provide, on the one hand, a more stable movement of the phone and, on the other hand, information about the joints of the robotic arm that can always provide an absolute measurement value, which does not accumulate errors over time.

Sensor Fusion

By employing graph optimization, multimodal information can be fused to improve the estimation of the phone's trajectory, which in turn leads to more accurate point cloud registration models (figure 5).

Post processing

After registering the high precision point cloud, the model can be simply converted to solid model though surface smoothing. The final model can be used for 3D printing. Finally, personalized earplugs can be made.
