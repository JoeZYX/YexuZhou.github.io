About Me

💻 To date, I have completed 12 collaborative projects with industry companies and academic institutions. My work spans various domains, including predictive maintenance, road damage detection, logistics optimization, traffic counting, object detection and tracking, medical image segmentation, and the development of LLM-based agents.

📊 My Ph.D. research is focused on creating lightweight, deployable deep learning models for wearable human activity recognition tasks. During my Ph.D., I have published in total 19 academic papers, with 9 accepted at Rank A and 5 at Rank B conferences.

What I’m good at?

Data Science

I specialize in Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), which includes fundamental tasks such as data preparation, cleaning, and feature engineering. Additionally, I conduct various statistical analyses, such as correlation tests and distribution tests, and provide thorough interpretations of the results. To effectively present data, I am proficient in using different data visualization tools. To support ongoing analysis and monitoring, I create interactive data exploration dashboards using Python. Based on the results of data exploration and project objectives, I excel in building models using machine learning and deep learning techniques, designing experiments, and establishing evaluation criteria. Furthermore, I am skilled in optimizing model parameters efficiently using optimization algorithms or automated machine learning frameworks. Additionally, I can incorporate multiple objectives into the training process to address specific project needs and optimize for multiple targets.

Deep Learning

Through accumulated experience in projects and research, I have a deep understanding of various neural network architectures, including Convolutional Networks, Recurrent Networks, Graph Networks, Transformer, Diffusion Model, Adversarial Models, and Generative Models, along with their various variants. I am also well-versed in deep learning programming tools, like PyTorch and TensorFlow. Additionally, I understand the significance of data quanlity for model training, and have experience in collecting, organizing data, and even building data collection tools. I am proficient in training and fine-tuning these models and have extensive knowledge in optimizing model architectures using Automated ML and Neural Architecture Search techniques. My areas of expertise include time series classification and forecasting and object recognition and tracking, and fine-tuning LLMs. To better deploy these models, I am familiar with compression techniques such as model quantization and model pruning, to deploy models on edge devices like MCUs.

Learning Ability
