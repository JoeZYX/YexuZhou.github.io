FreshIndex

The FreshIndex project, led by TSENSO, aims to address the challenge of food waste, where accurate data is needed to optimize the food supply chain and improve sustainability and safety due to the varying characteristics and shelf-life of food. FreshIndex provides an interconnected system for assessing and visualizing the true quality, safety, and shelf-life of food products, including dynamic best before dates that are based on real-world conditions.

Challenging

Sparsity of measurement: As it was in the initial phase of the project, the collected data were highly unsystematic, e.g., the fruits are measured at various times and locations, leading to a substantial amount of missing data. This necessitated the application of advanced data preprocessing techniques to either fill in these gaps or develop models capable of handling incomplete data.
Ultra-small-scale dataset: Due to the invasive nature of the measurement of ground truth values, which destroys the fruit, there is a scarcity of labeled data (less than 50 samples). To leverage also the unlabeled data, semi-supervised learning techniques need to be employed.
Prediction uncertainty and anomaly detection: For ML users, not only on the output, but also the confidence of the output is important. To achieve this, Bayesian methods are employed alongside other techniques to provide and enhance the confidence of the model outputs.

AutoEncoder based semi-supervised learning

By employing autoencoders and decoders, even unlabeled data can be utilized to train models that learn the intrinsic characteristics of the data. Meanwhile, another head is used for the regression task (the real objective), which makes the model learn useful data features for prediction making (figure 1).
In the autoencoder and decoder, multiple approaches to reduce and increase feature sizes are designed (figure 2).
Another advantage of autoencoders is that they can be used for anomaly detection. Because the error model between the output of the decoder and the input reflects the effectiveness of the model in learning the features of the input data.

Bayesian inference

By using a Bayesian approach, the network can give not only a prediction, but also a confidence level about the prediction. Moreover, by using Bayesian approach, the neural network can perform better with small datasets.

Hyperparameter Optimization

In the whole project, as numerous hyperparameters and network structures are proposed (figure 2, figure 4), we employed Bayesian optimization to find the best hyperparameters and network structures.
