Magnetic Tracking System

Magnetic tracking system is a method to track the position and orientation of an object by using magnetic field. It is widely used in medical field, such as surgical navigation, motion tracking, etc. In this project, we propose a magnetic tracking system from scratch.

Modeling of a magnet

According to Biot Savart law, the magnetic field generated by a magnet can be numerically calculated. The magnetic field of a cyclinder magnet is visualized in figure 1.

Specification of sensor array

Considering the strength of the magnet and the potential applications, we designed a 5x5 sensor array. The sensor array is composed of 16 Hall sensors, which are placed in a 5x5 grid. The distance between two adjacent sensors is 25mm.

Localization algorithm

For the localization algorithm, the objective function is defined as:

$$ \mathop{\rm minimize}_{P,R} \|B(P,R) - \tilde{B}\|^2, $$

where \(P\) and \(R\) are the position and rotation of the magnet, \(B(P,R)\) is the magnetic field generated by the magnet with pose \((P,R)\), and \(\tilde{B}\) is the magnetic field measured by the sensor array. As the rotation matrix \(R\) forms a spherical manifold, we use the Euler angle to represent it. The optimization problem is solved by the gradient method.

Hardware validation

Finally, the proposed method is validated by experiments. The magnet is placed above the sensor array, and the position and rotation of the magnet are estimated by the proposed method (figure 3).
