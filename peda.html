Printed Wearable Stress Detector

In the collaboration between TECO (professionalized in human-computer interaction and ubiquitous computing) and INT (specialized in nano-electronic technology), we designed, optimized and manufactured a flexible wearable electronic device for stress detection. The tiny device is composed of a printed electrodermal activity sensor, a printed band-pass filter as signal processor, and a printed diode-connected transistor as stress classifier (figure 1).
After the circuit design, the parameters, i.e., resistances and capacitances, as well as the threshold diode, are optimzied through CMA-ES algorithm To obtain best performance on the WESAD dataset (a famous dataset for wearable stress detection), see figure 2.
After optimizing the circuit parameters, they are printed on a flexible substrate and then tested within the labor environment (figure 3).
Subsequently, the device is measured on the human body, and the results are compared with the commercialized device (figure 4).
After some additional signal post-processing, the printed device can achieve comparable performance to the commercialized device (figure 5).
